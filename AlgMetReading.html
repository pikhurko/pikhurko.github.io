Algebraic Methods in Combinatorics:

Some suggestions for reading.


Here are some textbooks and articles that are related to the course.
  1. N. Alon, Tools from higher algebra, Handbook of Combinatorics (R. L. Graham, M.Grotschel, and L. Lovasz, eds.), Elsevier Science B.V., 1995, pp.1749-1785 (Moore Lib. QA164.H33)  
    [An excellent survey, with some proofs and many references. Almost everything what is there is related to the course, but unfortunately I'll have to omit much because lack of time.]

    2.  
  2. N. Alon, Combinatorial Nullstellensatz, Combinatorics, Probability and Computing 8 (1999) 7-29.

    3. [Contains some recent results not in the previous survey.]
     
  3. L. Babai and P. Frankl, Linear Algebra Methods in Combinatorics, Manuscript 1992. 
    [Unfortunately, this monograph has not been completed, let alone published. It is available from the the Department of Computer Science, University of Chicago (who charge about $30 for printing and shipping). I have deposited a copy in the Moore Library: QA184.B33 1992. Don't be scared by 200+ pages - it goes at a leisurely pace, with lots of good exercises. Chapters 2, 5, 6 are related.]

    4.  
  4. C. G. Godsil, Tools from linear algebra, Handbook of Combinatorics (R. L. Graham, M.Grotschel, and L. Lovasz, eds.), Elsevier Science B.V., 1995, pp.1705-1748 (Moore Lib. QA164.H33). 
    [Sections 2 & 4.]

  5. M. Aigner and G. M. Ziegler, Proofs from The Book, Springer 1998 (Moore Lib. QA36.A36 1998 & QA36.A36 2000). 
    [A hightly entertaining book (general mathematics, not only combinatorics). Read Chapter 15 on Borsuk's conjecture. Also, Chapter 28 on the Shannon capacity might be related.]
